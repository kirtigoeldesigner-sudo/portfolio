01 — The Problem & Context

A century-old method
in a modern factory

Manufacturers still relied on century-old time-and-motion study practices to measure manual processes. This methodology is suitable for incremental improvements — but modern manufacturers need to exponentially accelerate cost reduction, quality improvement, operator training, and new product introductions.

Drishti Technologies created a state-of-the-art platform that analysed production video on assembly lines using ML, providing previously unavailable data about manual processes — e.g. exact time taken for each operator action per cycle. This unlocked real-time quality and productivity insights, and operator training capabilities.

The UX team's mandate: identify critical flows to help customers successfully onboard Drishti and have users engage with it effectively day-to-day.

Heuristic Evaluation — Key Findings
Finding 01
Manufacturing entity not visible
Portal navigation was action-pivoted rather than entity-pivoted. Users expected to navigate by Line or Station — not by feature action.
Finding 02
Home screen lacked dynamism
The home screen showed information that remained largely static for 6 months, disconnected from the live nature of the system.
Finding 03
Statistical, not contextual
Visualisations were purely statistical in nature, lacking overview and operational context — making them inaccessible for non-industrial engineers.
02 — Understanding the System & User

20+ interviews and a factory visit

A manufacturing assembly line has several stations on which operators execute pre-defined, pre-trained actions. Real-world challenges make manufacturing far messier than the theory suggests. After speaking with 20+ industrial engineers — including customer-facing teams — and visiting a factory, we mapped the flow of roles, hierarchies, data, and processes.

01
Strong Hierarchy
Production line roles must deliver precise metrics to superiors on a daily shift basis. Information flows strictly upward — and quickly.
02
Near Real-Time Decisions
Root-cause analysis cannot be deferred. Even a few hours of delay makes findings irrelevant. Time is the most critical constraint.
03
User Fatigue is Real
Repetitive operations in a strict environment are mentally and physically tiring. Interfaces need to be effortless — reducing cognitive load, not adding to it.

03 — Workflows & Process

Mapping what exists vs.
what's possible

We mapped current and future flows in Drishti's customer journey and created detailed product user flows and information architectures. Onboarding was the most complex — a multi-part flow spanning factory setup, ML video training, and AI inference requests, none of which had been fully solved.

Step 01
Factory Setup
Input received via multiple communication channels, files, and manual entry — a fragmented process ripe for error.
Step 02
ML Video Dataset
ML video training finalised through back-and-forth clarifications over emails and files — with no in-product tracking.
Step 03
Request for AI
Inference frequency and delivery details were under-specified in SOWs, causing repeated clarification loops.
Step 04
Usability Testing
Each workflow iterated through structured usability sessions with actual industrial engineers and customer teams.

  • 3

01 — Context & Problem

Digital banking's tipping point
in India

With low data tariffs and increasing digital security, mobile banking picked up rapidly in India through the 2010s. UPI transformed payments — and checking account balance became the primary use case for mobile banking. Opening accounts became swift; digital banking moved mainstream.

Axis Bank is India's 3rd largest bank with 5,000+ branches. With digital banking's rise and use cases like fund transfer, bill payments, and credit card payments becoming prevalent, Axis needed a modern, contemporary mobile experience to match the quality users expected from fintech apps.

Heuristic Evaluation Findings
Navigation Chaos
90+ menu items with poor grouping. Navigation spread across pre-login, side menu, and post-login — including a scrollable side menu with no discoverability.
Security Perception
Quick login on the pre-login screen allowed users to view account balance without an mPIN — perceived as a security risk even if technically safe.

Existing Axis Mobile screens — pre-redesign heuristic evaluation

Setting the north star for a
trustworthy experience

While use cases and high-level flows were well-defined, the challenge was making the experience feel modern and contemporary — comparable to the best payments and delivery apps of the era. We defined goals by talking to executives, then validated usability goals with actual mobile banking users.

Usability Goals

Design Principles
◈
Security & Trust
Every interaction must reinforce user confidence. No ambiguous security states — clarity in authentication, permissions, and data visibility.

Users should feel in command of their finances. Show the system's status, surface relevant information, and minimise cognitive friction.
◇
Modern & Progressive
The interface should feel contemporary — matching the standard of the best consumer apps — while staying distinctively Axis.

01 — The Challenge

When interfaces fail,
pipelines don't forgive

The challenge was to design a next-generation experience for gas extraction monitoring, facility troubleshooting, and a coherent visual system for situation-aware HMIs — interfaces used in physically demanding, safety-critical environments.

The existing applications were built on older HTML technologies and showed minimal relevant information. They failed to account for environmental conditions — bright sunlight, outdoor HMI panels, glare — and provided little operational context beyond raw numbers.

Existing Experience — Shortfalls
Outdated Technology
Interfaces built with older HTML — not suited for modern HMI displays or varying screen sizes in field environments.
No Environmental Consideration
Screens not designed for outdoor viewing — failing in direct sunlight, on HMI panels mounted on facilities.
Context-Free Data
Monitoring interfaces showed raw metrics without situational context, making fast, safe decisions difficult under operational pressure.

02 — Understanding the System

Mapping entities in a
complex industrial environment

We spoke with stakeholders to understand the various entities in the gas extraction and transmission systems, how they related to each other, and how operators interacted with them in the field. We then mapped these relationships formally into an information architecture.

Two systems were in scope: the gas extraction facility (tanks, wells, operator stations) and the gas transmission facility — where an analyser interface needed to be designed for monitoring gas composition in transit.

03 — Visual Language & Designs

Designed for outdoors,
stress, and split-seconds

We defined a visual design language specifically suited to the rugged outdoor environment of oil and gas operations. High contrast, clear status hierarchies, embedded alarm signals, and a quick-glance interface structure were non-negotiable requirements — not nice-to-haves.

We delivered detailed designs across various scenarios and screen sizes, working closely with ABB's development team to ensure hand-off precision.
